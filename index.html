Our Journey

It was the year 1947. India gained independence. The country was partitioned. Bearing the agony of being uprooted, waves of people, rendered homeless, migrated from East Bengal to West Bengal. Thus began a new struggle for survival. It took several years to secure adequate food, clothing, and shelter. Once a semblance of stability was achieved, they felt the necessity to arrange for the education of the future generation.

Consequently, schools began to spring up in various places. Most of these institutions were built through the initiative and financial contributions of local residents. Similarly, a few education enthusiasts of our Sripur village first established the Sripur Primary School. Then, exactly 50 years ago today, on February 2, 1968, Sripur Junior High School was established. It started with classes 5 and 6, totaling 11 students and three teachers who served without salary. The school will forever remember these students and teachers.

Our school received official recognition for classes 5 and 6 in 1974. Classes 7 and 8 were subsequently introduced. Students from several neighboring villages began to gather in our school premises. Thereafter, receiving government affiliation up to class 8 in 1980 and up to the Secondary (Madhyamik) level in 2000, it was upgraded to Sripur High School.

Behind this brief summary lies a vast history. We respectfully acknowledge the contribution of the villagers of Sripur in shaping this history. We are indebted to all the teachers who, since the school's inception, have taught students with dedication either without remuneration or for a nominal fee. We have endless gratitude for the guardians of the students, whose donations have gradually contributed to the expansion and beautification of the school building.

We are grateful to the respected late MP Chitta Basu, respected MP Dipankar Mukherjee, and respected MP Sarala Maheshwari. They assisted in the construction of our school building using funds from their MP quotas. We are also grateful to the MLA of Madhyamgram, Sri Rathin Ghosh. He extends his helping hand whenever we are in need.

Sripur Primary School—which we consider a part of ourselves—has always stood by us in times of need. Ignoring their own inconveniences, they allowed us to use their building during the construction of ours. We do not wish to distance them by merely offering formal thanks.

Almost all students of our school come from low-income families. Due to various reasons, they have very limited opportunities to study at home. We can proudly say that we have consistently maintained the standard of education in our school. We ensure there is no lack of dedication in teaching our students.

On this joyous day, we especially remember our three colleagues—respected Anima Sengupta, respected Ranjit Roy Chowdhury, and respected Manoj Banerjee. The contribution of Anima Sengupta, from the school's inception to her last working day, is unforgettable. We will forever respectfully remember the contributions made by our former Headmaster Ranjit Roy Chowdhury and our colleague Manoj Banerjee toward the development and progress of the school. These three are not amongst us today, but their invisible, luminous presence continues to inspire us.

Today is a day of great joy for us. Through everyone’s collective effort, our school has secured a significant place in the educational landscape of Madhyamgram. We hope that with your cooperation, we will be able to progress even further in the future. The mantra with which we were initiated fifty years ago when our journey began, is the same mantra we hold dear today —

"Tamaso Ma Jyotirgamaya"
(Lead us from darkness to light)
